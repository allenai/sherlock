A Short How-To for "Find clues and make observations!"

After a careful examination of the responses, I am realizing that the boxing mechanism is not as easy as I had expected. We have had some responses where ALL boxes are of the same number. So here is a short visual tutorial. Key with his HIT is to do them in sequence. (Also, apologize for minor errors/typos in this tutorial, as am trying to push it out quickly.)

PART 1

STEP 1:
1A Note observation number 1 is chosen for you in the drop box
1B write down your observed clues in the text field to the right. (What you write here will be transferred over to the PART 2 below.)
STEP 2:
Draw bounding boxes in the image below. The boxes do not have to be perfect!
STEP 3A:
Choose observation number 2 from the drop down box.
STEP 3B:
Write observed clue for observation # 2 in the text field to the right (What you write here will be transferred over to the PART 2 below.)
STEP 3C:
Draw bounding boxe(s) observed clue(s)
STEP 3D:
Repeat for observation # 3 (and other bonus ones)

NOW we move on to PART 2!

STEP 1:
  • Note observed clues are filled in for you!
  • write in the indications
  • make a likelihood choice on the strength of your confidence
STEP 2:
  • write in the indications for the rest of the fields
  • make a likelihood choice on the strength of your confidence for the rest of the observations
I hope you found this tutorial clear. If you so desire, there is a sample template you can play with here. Please don't worry about messing it up because it really won't do anything (but error -- but error means nothing here).

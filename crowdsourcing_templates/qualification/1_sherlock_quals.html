Notes about qualifier

  • In this qualifier, you will be presented with 3 sets of clues/indications to annotate. In the actual batches, please expect to see one per HIT.
  • We expect this qualifier to take about 7-9 minutes.
  • Please read the instructions and the examples carefully.
  • If you are among the few who have worked on the pilot for task, make sure you still read the instructions as there are some key differences from the pilot.
Your task:

In this task, we are asking you to put on your detective thinking cap. Given an image, find observable clues that might indicate information about a person, situation, or setting that may not be necessarily obvious in the image (we will call this indication).

  • Examine the image and find 3 observable clues.
    ↳ An observable clue MUST be something in the picture (e.g., an open algebra math workbook)
  • For each observable clue, provide a indications.
    ↳ An indication is a bit of non obvious information about what the clue means to you (e.g., an open algebra math workbook might indicate there might be a high school student who was just studying )
  • Rate how likely for the indications to be true given the clue
    • highly likely: It's obvious or i'm very much certain what I said is true.
    • likey:There's a fairly good chance that what I said is true.
    • possible: It's in the realm of possibily but it's an educated guess at best.
  • Provide 1-2 sentence description of the image

Five examples are given below the instruction panel.

Rules:
  • For observable clues:
    • Write a noun phrase: "the book", "gray skies", "a group of people"
    • note! When possible, specify as to where the object, entity, or thing is located:
      • "the book" → "the book under the table"
      • "buttons" → "buttons on the man's shirt"
      • "a group of people" → "a group of people in the pool"
      • "a painting" → "a painting hanging on the wall"
    • You can provide the same observable clue multiple times (see Example 2).
  • For indications:
    • Write in complete sentences
    • note! Make the indications/deductions realistic
  • At this time, we are NOT interested in:
    • plain description of what's obviously going on
    • what people are doing in the picture
    • what people are thinking, wanting or desiring
    • what they might be immediately planning to do
  • Rather than a direct description like those above, stick to information that might indicate something interesting about a person, situation or setting. For example:
    • In a picture woman holding an icecream
      NO: ice cream - the woman is wanting to eat it
      Instead TRY: ice cream in woman's hand - It is a hot day
    • In a picture of a growling dog and man hugging a child
      NO: man - the man is being protective of the child
      Instead TRY: dog growling from backyard - The dog is dangerous
  • Please avoid gendered pronouns like "he", "she", "him" or "her". If you desire, you can use "they".
  • Read through examples!
Some questions to help get you thinking:
  • Why what's happening is happening? Any indicators for the reasons?
  • Think about the location. Where is this? How can you tell?
  • What activity just happened. How can you tell?
  • What does the image say about the people in the photos, people related to the photos or person taking the photo?
  • When is this happening? Christmas time? Afternoon tea?
